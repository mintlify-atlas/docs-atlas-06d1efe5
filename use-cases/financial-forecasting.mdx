## Overview

Financial forecasting with MiroFish leverages multi-agent simulation to model complex market dynamics that traditional statistical methods often miss. By simulating thousands of investors, traders, analysts, and institutional players with diverse strategies and risk tolerances, MiroFish can predict market reactions to events, policy changes, and economic signals.

<Note>
This use case is valuable for investment firms, financial analysts, risk managers, and economic researchers.
</Note>

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Market Scenario">
    Upload seed data about your forecasting scenario:
    - Financial reports and earnings statements
    - Economic indicators and policy announcements
    - Market sentiment data from news and social media
    - Historical price and volume data
    - Analyst reports and forecasts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Build Market Graph">
    MiroFish constructs a knowledge graph containing:
    - **Assets**: Stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies
    - **Entities**: Companies, central banks, regulatory bodies
    - **Relationships**: Correlations, dependencies, competitive dynamics
    - **Events**: Earnings calls, policy decisions, market shocks
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate Market Participants">
    The system creates diverse agent profiles:
    
    **Retail Investors**
    - Various risk tolerances and time horizons
    - Different information sources and biases
    - Emotional and momentum-driven behavior
    
    **Institutional Traders**
    - Algorithmic and fundamental strategies
    - Large position sizes affecting market impact
    - Access to premium information and analysis
    
    **Market Makers**
    - Liquidity provision and spread management
    - Inventory and risk management
    
    **Analysts & Influencers**
    - Research publication and recommendations
    - Social media presence and follower networks
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Run Market Simulation">
    Agents interact in a simulated marketplace:
    - Place orders based on their strategies and signals
    - React to price movements and news
    - Share opinions and analysis on social platforms
    - Update beliefs based on market feedback
    - Adjust positions and risk exposure
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Extract Insights">
    The ReportAgent analyzes outcomes:
    - Price trajectory predictions
    - Volatility and risk forecasts
    - Key price levels and support/resistance
    - Sentiment shifts and their market impact
    - Identifying likely market scenarios
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use Rich Market Data">
    Include historical price data, volume patterns, and sentiment indicators in your seed data. More context leads to more accurate agent behavior modeling.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Calibrate Agent Distribution">
    Match your agent distribution to real market composition. For example, institutional traders may be fewer in number but control larger positions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Run Multiple Scenarios">
    Don't rely on a single simulation. Run multiple scenarios with different assumptions to understand the range of possible outcomes.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Validate with History">
    Test your simulations against historical events to validate that agents behave realistically.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Monitor API Costs">
    Financial simulations with many agents can consume significant API resources. Start with smaller simulations (40-60 rounds) before scaling up.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Typical Workflow

1. **Define the prediction question** (e.g., "How will the market react to Q3 earnings?")
2. **Gather seed data** from financial reports, market data, and sentiment sources
3. **Configure agent distribution** to match relevant market participants
4. **Set up event triggers** for the scenario you want to test
5. **Run baseline simulation** to establish expected outcomes
6. **Test variations** by injecting different variables or news
7. **Analyze results** focusing on price ranges, volatility, and sentiment
8. **Interview agents** to understand trading rationales
9. **Generate investment insights** and risk assessments

## Limitations and Considerations

<Warning>
While MiroFish provides valuable insights, it should complement—not replace—traditional financial analysis:
- Simulations are models, not guarantees of future performance
- Agent behavior is based on LLM understanding, which may not capture all market nuances
- Start with paper trading or small positions when acting on simulation insights
- Use MiroFish as one input among many in your decision-making process
</Warning>
