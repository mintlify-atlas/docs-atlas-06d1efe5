## Overview

Public opinion analysis with MiroFish enables you to predict how communities will react to events, policies, or news before they happen. By simulating social media interactions among thousands of AI agents with diverse viewpoints and behaviors, you can forecast sentiment trends, identify potential controversies, and prepare strategic responses.

<Note>
This use case is ideal for PR teams, government agencies, brand managers, and researchers studying social dynamics.
</Note>

## Real-World Example: Wuhan University Public Opinion

One of MiroFish's most compelling demonstrations involves predicting public opinion around events at Wuhan University. This simulation showcased how the platform can accurately forecast sentiment trends and community reactions.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload Seed Data">
    Provide your source materials such as:
    - News articles or press releases
    - Social media posts and comments
    - Public opinion reports
    - Historical sentiment data
    
    MiroFish accepts documents in various formats including text, PDF, and structured data.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Graph Construction">
    MiroFish automatically:
    - Extracts key entities (people, organizations, events)
    - Identifies relationships and social networks
    - Maps sentiment patterns and opinion clusters
    - Builds a GraphRAG representation of the social landscape
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Agent Generation">
    The system creates diverse agent profiles representing:
    - **Different demographics**: Age groups, educational backgrounds, professions
    - **Varied viewpoints**: Supporters, critics, neutral observers
    - **Behavioral patterns**: Active posters, passive readers, influencers
    - **Memory systems**: Long-term memory of past events and interactions
    
    Each agent has a unique personality and posting style.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Simulation Execution">
    Agents interact on simulated social media platforms:
    - Post original content and reactions
    - Engage in discussions and debates
    - Form opinion clusters and influence networks
    - React to new information and trending topics
    
    The simulation runs for configurable time periods (e.g., 24-72 hours of activity).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Analysis & Reporting">
    The ReportAgent analyzes simulation results to provide:
    - Sentiment trend predictions
    - Key opinion leader identification
    - Potential controversy detection
    - Recommended response strategies
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Typical Workflow

1. **Collect seed data** from news sources, social media, or existing reports
2. **Upload to MiroFish** via the web interface or API
3. **Configure simulation** parameters (agent count, duration, platforms)
4. **Run simulation** and monitor progress in real-time
5. **Review insights** from the generated prediction report
6. **Interview agents** to understand different perspectives
7. **Test scenarios** by injecting new variables (e.g., different PR responses)

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use Diverse Seed Data">
    Include multiple viewpoints and sources in your seed data to ensure the agent population reflects real-world diversity. This improves prediction accuracy.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Start with Moderate Rounds">
    Begin with 30-50 simulation rounds to test your setup. Complex scenarios may require 100+ rounds but consume more API resources.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Define Clear Prediction Goals">
    Be specific about what you want to predict (e.g., "Will this policy announcement be well-received by students?") to help MiroFish focus its analysis.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Interview Key Agents">
    Use the agent interview feature to dig deeper into why certain opinions formed, revealing insights not visible in aggregate statistics.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Advanced Features

### Dynamic Variable Injection

Test different scenarios by injecting variables mid-simulation:
- Release a clarifying statement
- Introduce a counter-narrative
- Simulate a response from authorities
- Add breaking news developments

### Multi-Platform Analysis

Simulate interactions across different social media platforms to see how opinion spreads and transforms across different communities.

### Temporal Analysis

Track how quickly opinions form and spread, identifying the critical first hours or days when intervention is most effective.
