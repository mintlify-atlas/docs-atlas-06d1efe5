## Overview

Policy testing with MiroFish allows decision-makers to rehearse the future before committing to real-world changes. By simulating how diverse populations of agents respond to new policies, regulations, or strategic decisions, you can identify unintended consequences, optimize implementation strategies, and prepare for public reactions—all without real-world risk.

<Note>
This use case is essential for government agencies, corporate strategy teams, non-profits, and any organization making decisions that affect communities.
</Note>

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Policy Scenario">
    Upload comprehensive background information:
    - Current policy or baseline conditions
    - Proposed policy changes or new initiatives
    - Relevant demographic and socioeconomic data
    - Historical precedents and case studies
    - Stakeholder positions and interests
    - Related news, reports, and analysis
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Build Context Graph">
    MiroFish constructs a knowledge graph capturing:
    - **Stakeholders**: Affected populations, interest groups, institutions
    - **Relationships**: Power dynamics, dependencies, alliances
    - **Resources**: Economic factors, infrastructure, information flows
    - **Context**: Historical background, cultural factors, existing tensions
    - **Policy Details**: Specifics of the proposed change
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate Diverse Agents">
    Create a representative population of agents:
    
    **Demographic Diversity**
    - Age groups and generational differences
    - Socioeconomic backgrounds
    - Geographic locations (urban/suburban/rural)
    - Educational levels
    
    **Interest Group Representation**
    - Direct beneficiaries of the policy
    - Those negatively impacted
    - Industry stakeholders
    - Advocacy organizations
    - Government officials and implementers
    
    **Behavioral Variation**
    - Political leanings and ideologies
    - Risk tolerance and change receptivity
    - Information sources and media diets
    - Social influence and network position
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Simulate Policy Implementation">
    Agents interact as the policy unfolds:
    - React to policy announcement
    - Discuss with peers and on social platforms
    - Adjust behaviors in response to the policy
    - Form coalitions and organize responses
    - Interact with institutions and authorities
    - Share experiences of policy impact
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Analyze Outcomes">
    The ReportAgent evaluates:
    - Overall public acceptance vs. resistance
    - Compliance rates and implementation challenges
    - Unintended consequences and side effects
    - Demographic variation in responses
    - Optimal communication and rollout strategies
    - Potential flashpoints and controversies
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Features

### Multi-Phase Simulation

Model policy implementation across distinct phases:
1. **Announcement**: Initial public reaction
2. **Consultation**: Feedback and revision period
3. **Preparation**: Pre-implementation adjustments
4. **Launch**: Initial rollout
5. **Maturation**: Long-term adoption and adaptation

### Scenario Comparison

Run parallel simulations to compare:
- Different policy formulations
- Various communication strategies
- Alternative implementation timelines
- Different levels of stakeholder engagement

### Dynamic Policy Adjustment

Test mid-course corrections:
- Inject policy modifications during simulation
- Test response to clarifications or amendments
- Model the impact of addressing concerns

### Cross-Cultural Modeling

Simulate the same policy in different contexts:
- Geographic variations (different regions or cities)
- Cultural differences (international policy transfer)
- Demographic variations (different age groups or communities)

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Represent All Stakeholders">
    Ensure your agent distribution includes everyone significantly affected by the policy, not just the most vocal groups. Silent majorities can surprise you.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use Realistic Baseline Data">
    Ground your simulation in actual demographic data, opinion polls, and social science research about your target population.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Define Success Criteria Upfront">
    Be clear about what constitutes successful policy adoption. Is it 60% approval? 80% compliance? No major controversies?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Model Implementation Reality">
    Include the practical challenges of implementation (resource constraints, institutional capacity, etc.), not just the policy on paper.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Interview Diverse Agents">
    Use the agent interview feature to understand perspectives from different demographic and interest groups, especially those you might not personally relate to.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Run Sensitivity Analysis">
    Test how robust your policy is by varying assumptions and parameters. Policies that succeed in many scenarios are more resilient.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Typical Workflow

1. **Identify the policy decision** requiring analysis
2. **Gather context materials**: Current policies, stakeholder analysis, relevant data
3. **Define policy alternatives** to compare
4. **Map stakeholder groups** and their likely positions
5. **Configure agent distribution** to match real population
6. **Run baseline simulation** with current policy
7. **Simulate proposed policy** with same agent population
8. **Compare outcomes** between baseline and proposal
9. **Test variations** to optimize the policy
10. **Interview agents** to understand objections and support
11. **Generate recommendations** for implementation strategy
12. **Share findings** with decision-makers

## Ethical Considerations

<Warning>
Policy simulation is powerful but must be used responsibly:

- **Transparency**: Be clear when decisions were informed by simulation
- **Inclusivity**: Ensure agent populations represent all affected groups, especially marginalized voices
- **Limitations**: Simulations are models, not guarantees. Always gather real stakeholder input too
- **Bias awareness**: LLMs may reflect societal biases. Validate findings against real data
- **Privacy**: Avoid including personal data in seed materials
- **Democratic values**: Use simulations to improve policy, not to manipulate public opinion
</Warning>
